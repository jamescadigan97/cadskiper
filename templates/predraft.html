Pre-Draft Power Rankings


Rank Team Comments
1 Cole Last year was a rough year for Cole. Statistically, he did fine to start copping expected wins even though he wasn't getting actual wins. His team fell flat in the second half. Partially due to injuries, partially do to people running out of gas. He still comes in as next year's favorite to win the title. Don't expect to see him in week 15 with his boom or bust nature - he'll either have a bye or miss the playoffs.
2 David's Balls In David's Balls two years in the league, he has been nothing but the most consistent team. He has gotten a first round bye every year, took a trip to the finals his rookie year and won the regular season scoring title last year. He'll stay near the top of the power rankings unitl further notice because everytime you sleep on him, he pops.
3 Falcon Over the years, Falcon has 3 finals appearances but no championships. Has he shown the ability to be clutch? Not so far. But he's almost always a contender. Falcon has shown the most consistent of anyone that's been in the league more than 2 years. He's repeatedly shown that he can get late into the postseason, but the question remains whether he can get over the championships hump.
4 Dooley Someone forget to tell Dooley that the fantasy league switched to a week 17 championship week. What happened? His players must have been more preoccupied booking tee times and planning vacations instead of getting fantasy points during championship week. Dooley had a strange season last year- consistent in the first part, dominate in the second half before the finals with a few STINKERS mixed in. He's been a contender 2 of the last 4 years and hopes once again to challenge for a title.
5 Tony Plush Ahhh the first of the 3 "one-year-wonders". Stikes is always relevant and seems to be a contender ... until Thanksgiving hits. You'd have to look all the way back in the history books to the Obama Administration to see him in the finals, long predating the Tony Plush days. Stikes is a staple in the playoffs, making it in 6/7 of his years in the league. Anything can happen in the playoffs, just look at the next guy...
6 Matty Ice The second of the "one-year-wonders". Can a sub .500 team win a champiosnship? Possible, but unlikely (empirically once in 7 years). Will Matt have another sub .500 regular season? Not saying he will, but he has for the past 4 years. In PPR, Matty Ice has also never finished the regular season above 5th. Last year he snuck into the playoffs but caught lightening in a bottle when it mattered the most. The odds are stacked against Matty Ice to defend his title, but I'm not going to discount the possiblity.
7 David Bowie came in hot to the league with a strong finals run to start off his career in Milton fantasy. Since then however he's cooled off, with barely missing the playoffs next year and a poor showing in the quarterfinals last year. Over the years, David has shown a consistent floor with contending level upside.
8 SZN No longer a "new" member to the league, SZN needs to start putting up production. In his 4 years in the league, he only has 1 playoff win. Last year SZN looked to be a threat until a Henry injury derailed his season. Look for SZN to bounce back this year, but his lack of prior playoff success makes it harder to make a case for him year after year.
9 Bucci Believe it or not, Bucci's name is there among the winners. It may be in hieroglyphics, but there nonetheless Bucci has the ablility to turn back the time machine into his dominant days, but until then he'll dwell in the home he has made in the bottom of the rankings.
10 Stocktown Stocktown's only year of success is tainted with controversy. No longer Cole's student, Sam has a reason for optimism this year. The main question for Sam is focus. It seems like he manages his team from a different country every year. If Sam can put in the time he'll turn it around, but he needs to show something this season.


Note on the rankings - these rankings are mostly concerned with the likelihood to win championships.
