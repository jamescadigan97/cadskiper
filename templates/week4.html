Week 4 Power Rankings

Before we get into the rankings, here is the official 9ths (expected win) report through 4 weeks, courtesy of our league historian Cole "Stats" O'Flaherity.


The 9ths show that Matty Ice and Oasis are overperforming compared to what they should be, while Dooley and David's Balls are underperforming. SZN's hot start is backed up by the 9ths.


Rank Team Record Rank Change Comments
1 SZN 3-1 - - - - This is shaping up to be SZN's season. Boasting both an elite runningback room and wide recievers corp, this is the team to beat. In my predraft ranking, I said this was a "prove-it" year for SZN, and so far he has done that. Exciting times to be a duck.
2 Playoffs or Retire 3-1 - - - - I was on Bucci's case all last year and preseason and he responded by coming out of the gate hot. With an explosive lineup throughout, almost all of his players are liable to pop. The 9ths and points for back up Bucci's impressive start, look for Bucci to not have to retire.
3 JuHM 3-1 - - - - JuHM wraps out what I consider to be the three contenders right now. Since week 2, JuHM has been one of the most consistent teams in a year of low scoring. JuHM team has solid WRs and RBs but is especially strong at the one-player (QB, TE, DEF) roster spots.
4 Triple T 2-2 - - - - The strength of Triple T's WR corp has never been questioned, but there have been some growing pains around runningback. Fortunately, Dobbins is back and Triple T has a free week against the Toast Team to figure stuff out. Look for Triple T to become a stronger team in the second half of the year.
5 Matty Ice 3-1 - - - - Much like Triple T, Matty Ice boasts a solid WR corp, though not to the same extent. Unlike Triple T, he doesn't seem to have same forseeable answers at the runningback position. Recordwise Matty Ice started strong, but the expected wins say that his team isn't as strong as his records suggest.
6 Dooley 1-3 - - - - After 2 5 point losses, Dooley's lucky finally bounced back when Triple T decided to play himself. After 4 weeks, Dooley finds himself in middle of the pack terms of expected wins and in the top half of points for, despite a bottom barrel record. After a rough 0-3 start, Dooley made a couple deals potientially turn the direction of the season.
7 Oasis 3-1 - - - - Oasis got out to a 3-1 start despite middling runningbacks and a bare-bones wrs outside of his top guy. The expected wins scream overrated and being in the bottom 4 for points for suggests that there doesn't seem to be a lot of juice in the lineup. Fortunately, he has Stocktown and the Toast Team in 2 out of his next 3 games, so his record may not revert back to the way the advanced analytics see him.
8 David's Balls 1-3 - - - - After a dominate start to his career in the league, David's Balls has gotten to an uncharactoristically slow start. Bolstered by only his qb, top WR and runningback, David's Balls is gonna need to address a few depth issues before he can start thinking about contending.
9 The Toast Team 0-4 - - - - It didn't take long for the Crisp team to become the Toast team. While there have been a few close losses, the Crisp Team has been putting up unspiring performances. Neither the RBs or WRs are particulary strong. While the Crisp team is technically underperforming, it still is in the cellar for expected wins. Who knows though, Sir Purr started 0-4 and made the playoffs...
10 Stocktown 1-3 - - - - After being the league's top scorer week 1, Stocktown has failed to put on any relevant weeks since then. From weeks 2 and 3, he was the lowest scorer and week 4 he's only outscored Triple T and the Toast Team. Simply can't win games when you're routinely the lowest scoring team.


Overall I see a the top tier consisting of SZN, Bucci and JuHM. Then the next tier is TTT, Matty Ice and Dooley, who I see one or two pieces away from taking a step up. David's Balls and Oasis need a little more depth than the prior tier. Finally there's Toast and Stocktown.
